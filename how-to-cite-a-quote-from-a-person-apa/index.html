If you’re writing an academic paper, citing your sources is a crucial part of the writing process. But what happens when you want to quote someone directly? How do you properly cite that quote? In this blog post, we’ll be discussing how to cite a quote from a person APA style, so you can give credit where credit is due.

Pain Points Related to How To Cite A Quote From A Person APA

The process of citing a quote from a person in APA style can be confusing, especially if you’re new to citing sources. Improperly citing sources can lead to plagiarism, which can have serious consequences. It’s important to get it right.

The Target of How To Cite A Quote From A Person APA

The target of citing a quote from a person in APA style is to provide evidence for your argument while giving credit to the original source. By citing your sources, you are showing that you have done your research and are using credible sources to support your claims.

Main Points Related to How To Cite A Quote From A Person APA

When citing a quote from a person APA style, you should include the author’s name, the year the quote was made, and the page number (if applicable) in parentheses. If the quote is longer than 40 words, you should use a block quote, which is indented and does not use quotation marks. It’s also important to include a reference page at the end of your paper that lists all of the sources you used.

Personal Experience with Citing a Quote From a Person APA Style

When I was writing my thesis, I had to cite quotes from several different sources. At first, I found it confusing, but once I got the hang of it, it became easier. The key is to keep track of your sources as you go along, so you don’t have to go back and try to find them later. I also found it helpful to use citation tools like Citation Machine, which can generate citations for you.

Tips for Citing a Quote From a Person APA Style

If you’re new to citing sources, it can be helpful to use a style guide like the Publication Manual of the American Psychological Association (APA). This guide provides detailed instructions on how to format your citations and references. You can also use online citation tools like Citation Machine or EasyBib to generate citations for you.

### How To Cite a Quote From a Person APA Style in More Detail

When citing a quote from a person in APA style, you should include the author’s last name and the year the quote was made. If the quote is less than 40 words, it should be enclosed in quotation marks and followed by the author’s name and the year in parentheses. If the quote is more than 40 words, it should be indented, double-spaced, and not enclosed in quotation marks. For example:

According to Smith (2019), “the first step in solving any problem is admitting there is a problem” (p. 23).

Tips for Avoiding Plagiarism When Citing Quotes

When citing quotes, it’s important to avoid plagiarism by giving credit to the original source. Plagiarism can have serious consequences, including failing a course or even being expelled from school. To avoid plagiarism, make sure you are properly citing your sources and using quotation marks when necessary.

Question and Answer About How To Cite a Quote From a Person APA

Q: What if I can’t find the page number of the quote?

A: If you can’t find the page number of the quote, you can use a paragraph number (if available) or simply omit the page number altogether. In this case, you would just include the author’s name and the year of the quote in parentheses.

Q: What if I’m quoting a quote?

A: If you’re quoting a quote, you should indicate that in your citation. For example, if you’re quoting John Smith, who is quoting Jane Doe, your citation would look like this: (Smith, 2019, as cited in Doe, 2018).

Q: Do I need to cite every sentence in my paper?

A: No, you do not need to cite every sentence in your paper. You should only cite sources that you directly quote or paraphrase. However, if you are unsure whether or not you need to cite a source, it’s better to err on the side of caution and cite it anyway.

Q: Can I use footnotes instead of in-text citations?

A: Yes, you can use footnotes instead of in-text citations. However, you should check with your instructor to make sure footnotes are allowed in your specific assignment or paper.

Conclusion of How To Cite a Quote From a Person APA

Citing quotes from people in APA style can seem daunting at first, but with a little practice, it becomes much easier. By following the guidelines outlined in this post and using resources like style guides and citation tools, you can give credit where credit is due and avoid plagiarism.
