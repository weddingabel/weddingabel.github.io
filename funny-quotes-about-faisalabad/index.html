Funny Quotes About Faisalabad Explained

One funny quote that best portrays Faisalabad is “Faisalabadi ke tey Punjabiyat ne, terey dhande tey choti!” (Faisalabad’s Punjabi culture has diminished your profits). This quote showcases the hard-working and hard-hitting nature of the people of Faisalabad who are not afraid to speak their minds; and use humor to make their point. What makes it even more beautiful is the metaphor of Punjabiyat and dhande (business), as this is seen as verbal wit that is unique to Punjab and will strike a chord in the hearts of Punjabi-speaking people.

Another example is “Kya karonga main sehri mein, Faisalabad ke kabootar nahi hai jo roze rakhenge” (What will I do during sehri? I’m not a Faisalabad pigeon who will keep fasts). This quote highlights the love that the people of Faisalabad have for food. The quote is humorous and witty - remarking how people of Faisalabad love their food and can’t pass up a good meal.

Funny Quotes About Faisalabad and Hard Work

The people of Faisalabad are famously industrious, and their sense of humor is just as robust as their work ethic. “Faisalabadi se bohot suba ho gaye hai? Khud bhi kar lo kaam, dusro per burden mat daalo” (Are you tired of being a Faisalabadi? Do your own work, don’t be a burden on others) is a quote that highlights the city’s hard-working nature. It denotes self-respect and the willingness to put in the effort and hard work that Faisalabad is famous for.

Funny Quotes About Faisalabad’s Unique Charms

“Faisalabad mein aik cheez cheez ki dukaan hai jis se her chawal ki tarha miltaa julta hai” (There is a shop in Faisalabad where you can find everything, just like each grain of rice). The quote highlights the famous Dehli Gate, or “Chakki Waali Gali,” which has rows and rows of shops that sell everything under the sun. This quote shows the unique charm of Faisalabad and the amount of character it has.
