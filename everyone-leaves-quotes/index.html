Question and Answer

Q: How do I know when it’s time to let go?

A: Letting go can be one of the most difficult steps in the healing process. However, when we hold onto things that no longer serve us, we prevent ourselves from moving forward and living our best lives. One way to know when it’s time to let go is by asking yourself if holding onto this thing (person, situation, belief) is bringing you closer to your goals and values or further away.

Q: How can I forgive someone who has hurt me deeply?

A: Forgiveness is a process that takes time and patience. One way to start the process is by understanding that forgiving someone does not mean that you condone their behavior or that you have to forget what they did. It simply means that you are choosing to release the anger and resentment that you may be holding onto. Practice self-compassion and remind yourself that forgiveness is a gift you give to yourself, not the other person.

Q: Is it normal to still feel pain even after years have passed?

A: Yes, it is completely normal to still feel pain even after years have passed. Healing is not a linear process, and it can take time to work through our pain and come to a place of peace. Be patient with yourself and remember that healing is a journey, not a destination.
