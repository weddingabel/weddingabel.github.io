Deforestation and Climate Change

One of the most significant impacts of deforestation is its contribution to climate change. Trees absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere, and deforestation reduces the number of trees that can absorb this greenhouse gas. The result is an increase in atmospheric carbon dioxide levels, which contribute to climate change and its associated effects, such as rising sea levels and more frequent and severe natural disasters.

To address the issue of deforestation and protect our planet, we must promote sustainable development.
