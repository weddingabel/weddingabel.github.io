Do you ever find yourself quoting someone important, but not knowing how to properly cite their words? Proper citation of a quote is important in both academic and professional settings, but not everyone knows how to do it correctly. In this article, we’ll go through the steps of how to cite a quote from a person, whether it’s in an essay, report, or any other type of document.

Potential Confusion and Frustration

Citing a quote from a person can be frustrating if you don’t know how to do it. Without proper citation, you may run into trouble with plagiarism or not give proper credit to the original source. This can lead to confusion and frustration, not only for you, but also for anyone reading your work.

The Target of How To Cite A Quote From A Person

The target of this article is to provide a simple guide on how to cite a quote from a person and avoid any confusion or frustration in the process. With the proper steps and format, citing a quote can be easy and hassle-free.

Main Points To Remember

When citing a quote from a person, there are a few main points to remember. First, always provide the name of the person who said the quote. Second, include the date of the quote or the date when the quote was made. Third, provide the exact words that were spoken, including any errors or mistakes. Finally, make sure to properly format the citation according to the style guide recommended by your institution or organization.

Citing a Quote: Personal Experience

When I was writing my thesis, I found myself quoting several key individuals in my field. At first, I wasn’t sure how to properly cite their words, and it was causing me a lot of stress. However, after doing some research and consulting with my advisor, I learned the proper steps to take. Following these steps made citation much easier and gave me peace of mind knowing my work was properly sourced.

### Step-by-Step Guide for Citing a Quote

Here are the step-by-step guidelines for citing a quote from a person:

  1. Provide the name of the person who said the quote
  2. Include the date or time period of the quote
  3. Provide the exact words spoken, including any errors or mistakes
  4. Make sure to properly format the citation according to the style guide

#### More Details On Citing a Quote

When citing a quote from a person, it’s important to remember some additional details. For example, if the quote is from a speech, make sure to include the location and event where the speech was given. Additionally, if the quote is from a written work, make sure to include the page number where the quote was found. These additional details can help provide context and clarity to the quote.

Examples of Properly Cited Quotes

Here are some examples of properly cited quotes using different citation styles:

  • APA Style: “We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act, but a habit.” (Aristotle, 350 BCE/1991)
  • Chicago Style: “I have not failed. I’ve just found 10,000 ways that won’t work.” (Thomas Edison, diary entry, 19 August 1885)
  • MLA Style: “Two roads diverged in a wood, and I- I took the one less traveled by, And that has made all the difference.” (Robert Frost, “The Road Not Taken”)

Question and Answer

Q: Can I paraphrase the quote instead of citing it directly?

A: While paraphrasing can help add context or clarity to a quote, it’s always best to cite the quote directly if possible. This gives proper credit to the original source and avoids any issues with plagiarism.

### Q: What if I can’t find the date of the quote?

A: If you can’t find the date of the quote, try to provide an approximate time period or year when the quote was made. This can help provide context and ensure proper citation.

Q: What citation style should I use?

A: The citation style you use will depend on your institution or organization. Make sure to check with your professor or advisor to determine which citation style is recommended for your work.

Q: What if the quote has errors or mistakes?

A: When quoting a person, it’s important to provide the exact words spoken, even if there are errors or mistakes. However, you can indicate errors or mistakes by using “[sic]” after the error to show that it was not your mistake.

Conclusion of How To Cite A Quote From A Person

Citing a quote from a person may seem intimidating at first, but with the proper steps and format, it can be easy and hassle-free. Remember to always provide the name of the person, date of the quote, exact words spoken, and proper citation style. By following these guidelines, you can give proper credit to the original source and avoid any issues with plagiarism.
