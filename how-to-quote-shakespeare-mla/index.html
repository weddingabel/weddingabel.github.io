William Shakespeare is one of the most influential playwrights in history, known for his masterful use of language and poetic expression. As a student, scholar, or anyone interested in quoting Shakespeare in their writing, it’s important to know how to cite his works correctly in MLA format.

When quoting Shakespeare’s plays in MLA format, it’s important to include the act, scene, and line numbers of the quote, rather than the page number. For example, if you were quoting from Romeo and Juliet, Act 3, Scene 1, lines 87-90, your citation would look like this:

(Shakespeare 3.1.87-90)

How To Quote Shakespeare MLA

One of the easiest ways to remember how to correctly quote Shakespeare in MLA format is to use the acronym FACE:

  • First, write the full name of the author (William Shakespeare).
  • Next, include the name of the play in italics.
  • After the title of the play, include the act, scene, and line numbers in parentheses.
  • Finally, end the citation with a period.

Using this acronym, the citation for a quote from Macbeth in Act 2, Scene 1, lines 45-47 would look like this:

(Shakespeare Macbeth 2.1.45-47).

Common Mistakes

One common mistake when citing Shakespeare in MLA format is including the wrong edition of the play in your citation. It’s important to make sure you have the correct edition, as different editions may have different line numbers. Another common mistake is forgetting to add the period at the end of the citation.

FAQs About How To Quote Shakespeare MLA

Q: Should I include the entire quote in my paper?

A: No, you should only include the portion of the quote that is relevant to your argument.

### Q: How do I format a long quote from a play?

A: If the quote is three or more lines, you should start a new line and indent the quote one inch from the left margin. You do not need to use quotation marks.

### Q: How do I cite Shakespeare in my Works Cited page?

A: The citation should follow this format: Last Name, First Name. Title of Play. Publisher, Year of Publication.

Q: Should I use the same citation for every quote in my paper?

A: Yes, you should use the same citation format for every quote from the same play. However, if you are quoting from multiple plays, you will need to create separate citations for each.
