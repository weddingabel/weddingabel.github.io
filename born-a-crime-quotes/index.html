If you’re a fan of thought-provoking books, you’ve probably heard of “Born A Crime” by Trevor Noah. This autobiography dives into the complex racial dynamics of post-apartheid South Africa, all while telling the story of Noah’s childhood. But did you know that “Born A Crime” is also full of powerful and inspiring quotes? In this blog post, we’ll explore some of the most memorable quotes from the book and what makes them so impactful.

Pain Points

“Born A Crime” is a book that deals with heavy subjects like racism, discrimination, and poverty. While these themes are difficult to confront, they’re also incredibly important, especially in today’s world. By reading the book and reflecting on its quotes, readers may confront their own biases and prejudices, and hopefully come away with a greater understanding of how to combat systemic oppression.

Target of Born A Crime Quotes

The target of “Born A Crime” quotes is anyone who wants to be inspired by a story of resilience and hope in the face of adversity. The book’s quotes touch on themes like identity, family, and the power of laughter, and are relevant to people of all ages and backgrounds. Whether you’re a student struggling to make ends meet or an adult navigating the complexities of race and class, “Born A Crime” has something to offer you.

Main Points

In this blog post, we’ve discussed the pain points related to “Born A Crime” quotes, the book’s target audience, and why its quotes are so important. We’ve also touched on themes like resilience, hope, and the power of storytelling. Through the book’s quotes, readers may gain a greater understanding of their own lives, as well as the lives of others, and use that knowledge to effect positive change in their communities.

Born A Crime Quotes - Identity and Belonging

One of the most powerful quotes from “Born A Crime” is: “The most dangerous person in the world is a person who has nothing to lose.” For me, this quote speaks to the idea that when people are pushed to their limits - whether it’s due to poverty, discrimination, or other forms of oppression - they can either give up or fight back with a vengeance. This is something that I’ve witnessed time and time again in my own life, and it’s a testament to the strength of the human spirit.

Born A Crime Quotes - Family

Another memorable quote from “Born A Crime” is: “The genius of apartheid was convincing people who were the overwhelming majority to turn on each other. Apart hate, is what it was. You separate people into groups and make them hate one another so you can run them all.” This quote ties into the book’s larger themes of racism and discrimination, but it also speaks to the power of community and family. In times of adversity, it’s important to come together and support one another, rather than turning on each other.

### Born A Crime Quotes - Laughter

One of the things that makes “Born A Crime” so unique is its use of humor to offset some of the heavier themes. As Noah himself has said, “Laughter isn’t the opposite of sadness. It’s the opposite of depression.” In the book, Noah uses humor to cope with the challenges of growing up in South Africa, and to make sense of the chaotic world around him. This shows that even in the darkest of times, there is still room for joy and laughter.

#### Born A Crime Quotes - Racism and Discrimination

Finally, many of the quotes in “Born A Crime” touch on the themes of racism and discrimination. For example, “What I could not understand is why the color of your skin had anything to do with your ability to speak. Black people, in my experience, spoke better English than most of the white people I grew up with.” This quote illustrates the absurdity of using race as a way to judge someone’s intelligence or worth. By highlighting these issues, Noah encourages readers to question their own assumptions and biases, and to work towards a more just and equitable world.

Conclusion of Born A Crime Quotes

Overall, “Born A Crime” is a powerful and inspiring book that is full of thought-provoking quotes. By reading and reflecting on these quotes, readers may gain a greater understanding of the world around them and be inspired to effect positive change in their communities.
