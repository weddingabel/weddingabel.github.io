If you are a student or a writer, citing sources is an essential part of your work. The Modern Language Association (MLA) is the preferred citation style for the humanities, including literature, history, and philosophy. When citing a quote from an article in MLA format, there are specific rules you should follow to avoid plagiarism and give proper credit to the author.

Pain Points Related To How To Cite A Quote From An Article In MLA Format

It can be confusing to know what information to include when citing a quote from an article in MLA format. Some common pain points include not knowing how to format the in-text citation, how to properly include the author’s name, and how to cite the source on the works cited page. Additionally, it is sometimes unclear how to handle quotes that are longer than four lines. Not following MLA guidelines can result in lower grades or accusations of plagiarism.

Answer To How To Cite A Quote From An Article In MLA Format

When citing a quote from an article in MLA format, the in-text citation should include the author’s last name and the page number where the quote can be found. For example: (Smith 25). If the author’s name is already mentioned in the text, only the page number is necessary. If the quote is four lines or less, you can include it within the text and enclose it within quotation marks. For longer quotes, use a block quote, which should be indented one inch from the left margin and double-spaced. Finally, include the full citation on the works cited page, following the proper MLA format for the source type.

Main Points About How To Cite A Quote From An Article In MLA Format

When citing a quote from an article in MLA format, remember to:

  • Include the author’s last name and the page number in the in-text citation
  • Use quotation marks for quotes that are four lines or less
  • Use block quotes for longer quotes, indented one inch from the left margin and double-spaced
  • Include the full citation on the works cited page, following proper MLA format

How To Cite A Quote From An Article In MLA Format In More Detail

When citing a quote from an article in MLA format, it is important to use the correct punctuation and formatting. The in-text citation should come immediately after the quote, with no punctuation between them. The author’s last name should be included in the citation, usually in parentheses. If the author’s name is already mentioned in the text, only the page number is required.

The full citation should be included on the Works Cited page at the end of the document. The citation should include the author’s name, the title of the article, the title of the publication, the date of publication, and the page numbers. Make sure to follow the proper MLA format for the source type (book, article, website, etc.).

Question and Answer

1. What is the correct format for an in-text citation for a quote from an article?

The correct format for an in-text citation for a quote from an article is the author’s last name and the page number, usually in parentheses immediately following the quote.

2. How should you format a quote that is longer than four lines?

If the quote is longer than four lines, it should be formatted as a block quote, with one inch of indentation from the left margin and double-spaced.

3. Why is it important to cite sources properly?

It is important to cite sources properly in order to give credit to the original author, allow readers to find the source themselves, and avoid accusations of plagiarism.

4. What should be included in a Works Cited page entry for an article?

A Works Cited page entry for an article should include the author’s name, the title of the article, the title of the publication, the date of publication, and the page numbers.
